What we do
1 FRAMEWORK DEVELOPMENT
Develop personalized models of cycle sensitivity.
The DASH-MC framework, Dimensional Affective Sensitivity to Hormones, maps why some people are sensitive to estradiol (E2), progesterone (P4), or both, while most people have no sensitivities at all.  We think about hormone-related psychiatric symptoms as transdiagnostic, not disorder-specific.
Peters et al., 2024 · Molecular Psychiatry
2 MEASUREMENT & MODELING
Build better tools to study the menstrual cycle.
Our phase-aligned cycle time scaling (PACTS) approach, implemented in our open-source menstrualcycleR R package, realigns hormonal cycles across patients and months. Generalized additive models can be used to model average, subgroup, and person-specific cycle effects.
Nagpal et al., 2025 · Psychoneuroendocrinology
3 CLINICAL TRIALS
Test the mechanisms of different cycle sensitivities.
We pair randomized crossover hormone trials with real-time symptom reports, neuroimaging, and passive smartphone data to isolate how estradiol (E2) and progesterone (P4) each drive risk, revealing distinct biological pathways we can target.
Eisenlohr-Moul, 2025 · Am J Psychiatry
